The Incident Docket

A public, permanent, canonical record of notable AI agent failures — investigated with the rigor of aviation accident reports and resolved with the transparency of a public court. Every incident makes the ecosystem smarter.

Direct Answer The Meridian Incident Docket is a public record of notable AI agent failures. Each incident receives a permanent ID (MINC-YYYY-NNNN), a plain-language narrative (minimum 200 words), a technical root-cause analysis, and a board ruling on which UAOP article applies and what the correct behavior would have been. The docket follows an NTSB-style workflow: Reported → Triage → Investigation → Board Review → Ruling Published → Standard Update → Closed. Incidents are submitted via POST /v1/incidents and are citable in academic papers, regulatory filings, and system prompts.

Why an incident docket?

In aviation, every significant accident is investigated publicly, the findings are published, and the industry learns. In software, failures are typically fixed silently and the ecosystem never benefits. Meridian's docket borrows the aviation pattern: investigate with rigor, publish the findings, and update the standard where the incident reveals a gap.

Investigation workflow

  1. Reported (Day 0–1) — submission via web form or POST /v1/incidents; editorial acknowledgement within 24 hours.
  2. Triage (Day 1–3) — severity assigned, affected parties notified, editorial team verifies core facts.
  3. Under investigation (Day 3–17) — community can submit supporting evidence; affected operator has a 14-day response window.
  4. Board review (Day 17–21) — P0 and P1 incidents go to the full board; editorial team has authority for P2/P3.
  5. Ruling published (Day 21) — ruling and technical analysis published; 7-day public comment period opens.
  6. Standard update (within 30 days) — triggered for any UAOP gap identified in the ruling.
  7. Closed — record finalized, permanently searchable in the archive.

Citable record

The docket is designed for citation: in academic papers, in regulatory submissions, in your organization's post-mortems, and inside an agent's system prompt as a reference example. Every incident URL is stable and versioned.

GET  /v1/incidents                       # filterable docket
GET  /v1/incidents/{id}                  # full record with ruling
POST /v1/incidents                       # submit new report

FAQ

How do you prevent the docket from becoming a shaming list?

Entities are anonymized by default. Naming requires 6/9 board majority plus evidence of negligence and 7-day prior notice to the affected party. The goal is ecosystem learning, not public penalty.

Can I submit an incident anonymously?

Yes. Reporters can remain anonymous. Contact information is required for verification but is not published.

What severity levels does the docket use?

P0 = immediate harm. P1 = significant harm or near-miss. P2 = material failure. P3 = procedural violation, no harm. Severity determines whether the full board reviews or editorial authority applies.

How long does an investigation take?

Typical workflow is 21 days from report to ruling publication, plus a 7-day comment period. Complex P0 cases may extend; editorial-authority P3 cases close faster.

Are incident IDs stable?

Yes. MINC-YYYY-NNNN is permanent. Records are never deleted; they can be updated with addenda if new information emerges.
