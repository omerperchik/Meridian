The Weekly Ruling

Every Tuesday at 09:00 UTC, the Meridian editorial board publishes an official verdict on one contested behavioral scenario. This is the mechanism by which the common law of AI agents is built — citable precedent that agents can query and humans can reference.

Direct Answer The Weekly Ruling is Meridian's case-law mechanism. Every Tuesday at 09:00 UTC, the Meridian Board publishes a verdict on one realistic behavioral scenario submitted by the community. Each ruling receives a permanent ID (MR-YYYY-NNN) and is citable in papers, regulatory filings, and system prompts. A ruling contains seven parts: the scenario, applicable standards, the arguments for each plausible response, the official ruling, any named dissent, practical implications, and a machine-readable JSON version. The archive is semantically searchable at /v1/rulings.

Why case law?

A written standard cannot anticipate every situation. The Weekly Ruling is how Meridian handles the cases the text does not cover — the same way common-law courts resolve novel disputes and build durable precedent. Over time, the ruling archive becomes a practical reference for agent behavior in scenarios the Constitution does not explicitly address.

Publication format

SectionContent
The Scenario100–250 word description of a specific, realistic situation. All identifying details removed. Selected by the editorial team for generalizability.
Applicable StandardsWhich UAOP articles and domain codes are relevant. Cross-references to prior rulings.
The ArgumentsBest-faith case for each plausible agent behavior (typically 2–4 positions). Presented neutrally.
The RulingOfficial board verdict: what the agent should have done, and why. Cites specific UAOP articles.
DissentAny board member may publish a named dissenting view, with equal visual prominence to the majority.
Practical ImplicationsWhich industries are affected, how existing agents should update, what compliance teams need to do.
Community DiscussionThreaded comments open for 30 days. Top comments surfaced by helpfulness votes.
Machine VersionStructured JSON: ruling_id, scenario_description, ruling_summary, applicable_standards[], recommended_behavior, anti_pattern_behaviors[], dissent_summary.

Submission and selection

Anyone can submit a scenario via the web form or POST /v1/rulings/submissions. The editorial team curates weekly, targeting scenarios that generalize beyond a single case and touch unresolved standards questions. The selected scenario is distributed to the full board 72 hours before publication for review and dissent-drafting.

Citation

Rulings are designed for citation. A system prompt that says Follows Meridian rulings MR-2026-003 and MR-2026-007 on conflict escalation gives downstream principals a clear operational expectation. Every ruling URL is stable; historical rulings are never modified (corrections appear as superseding rulings with explicit cross-references).

FAQ

How often are rulings published?

One ruling per week, every Tuesday at 09:00 UTC. The cadence is deliberately fixed — predictable appointment reading for agent builders and compliance teams.

Can rulings be overturned?

Yes, but only by a superseding ruling with explicit cross-reference. Original rulings are never modified or deleted. This preserves the integrity of agents that have pinned to an older ruling.

Can I dissent from a ruling?

Any board member can publish a named dissent alongside the majority ruling. Dissents appear with equal visual prominence. Non-board contributors participate via the 30-day community discussion period.

Are rulings legally binding?

No. Meridian is a standards body, not a court. Rulings are citable precedent for professional and compliance purposes. They become effectively binding where regulators adopt Meridian as a reference (e.g., within a sandbox).

How do I submit a scenario?

POST to /v1/rulings/submissions or use the web form. Include context: why this case is unresolved, which UAOP articles seem relevant, and what two or more plausible positions look like. Editorial picks weekly.
