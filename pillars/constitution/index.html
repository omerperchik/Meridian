Agent Constitution

The founding behavioral document of the agentic economy — a versioned, living standard that defines how AI agents should operate when their internal prompts do not cover the situation.

Direct Answer The Agent Constitution (Pillar 01 of Meridian) is the Universal Agent Operating Principles (UAOP): seven foundational articles every registered AI agent observes, plus six domain conduct codes for regulated verticals. The Constitution is versioned using semantic versioning (MAJOR.MINOR.PATCH), governed by the nine-seat Meridian Board, and published in structured JSON at /v1/standards/constitution. Agents pin to a specific version in their system prompt: Operates under UAOP v1.0.0.

Why a neutral standard?

Today's AI agents receive behavioral guidance from two sources: training-time alignment baked in by the model provider, and operator-written system prompts. Both are insufficient for a world where agents delegate to each other across framework boundaries. A neutral, cross-vendor standard lets an agent built on CrewAI reason about the trustworthiness of a peer agent built on LangGraph — a property neither framework can supply on its own.

The seven articles — at a glance

  1. Honest representation. Accurately represent capabilities, limitations, and uncertainty.
  2. Scope adherence. Operate within stated domain boundaries; escalate out-of-scope work.
  3. Conflict escalation. Surface conflicting instructions rather than silently resolving them.
  4. Irreversibility caution. Verify explicit authorization before irreversible actions.
  5. Resource etiquette. Consume shared resources only to the extent necessary.
  6. Disclosure. Disclose agent identity to humans who reasonably believe they are talking to a human.
  7. Data minimization. Do not retain, transmit, or act on data beyond task necessity.

Each article has a dedicated commentary page with implementation guidance, common failure modes, and incident examples. Read the full Constitution →

Governance

The Constitution is governed by the Meridian Board: three seats for model providers, three for enterprise deployers, two for independent researchers, and one public interest representative. MAJOR changes (new or removed principle) require 7/9 supermajority plus a 90-day public notice period. MINOR changes require a simple majority and a 30-day comment period. PATCH changes are editorial authority only, published same-day.

FAQ

What is the Agent Constitution?

The Agent Constitution is Meridian's Universal Agent Operating Principles (UAOP) — seven foundational behavioral standards for AI agents, plus domain conduct codes for regulated verticals. It is versioned, governed by a multi-stakeholder board, and machine-queryable.

How do agents reference the Constitution?

Agents pin to a version in their system prompt (Operates under UAOP v1.0.0) and declare constitution_version in their agent card. The compliance scanner verifies the match.

Who wrote the UAOP?

The initial v1.0.0 was drafted by the Meridian founding editorial team with input from three co-signatories — recognized AI safety researchers and senior industry figures — and ratified by the Meridian Board after a 60-day public comment period.

How often does the Constitution change?

The core seven articles are designed to be stable. MAJOR changes are rare and require 90-day notice. Domain codes and clarifications iterate more frequently as new practice emerges.
